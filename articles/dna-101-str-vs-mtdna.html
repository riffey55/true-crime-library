DNA 101: STR vs. mtDNA

Forensics 101 Beginner Guide Last updated:

Overview

STR analyzes repeating nuclear DNA; mtDNA is maternal-line DNA that survives when STR may not. STR is standard for direct comparisons; mtDNA helps with degraded or tiny samples.

When to use which?

  • STR: Good, non-degraded samples; CODIS-compatible.
  • mtDNA: Hair shafts, bones, old/degraded samples; maternal line only.

Strengths & limits

  • STR strengths: High discrimination, validated.
  • STR limits: Degrades with time/heat; mixtures are complex.
  • mtDNA strengths: Survives harsh conditions.
  • mtDNA limits: Lower discrimination; can‚Äôt distinguish maternal relatives.
